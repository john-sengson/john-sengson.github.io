Sprint 4 - Technical


Tell us about a time when you got block on a single problem:

Just this week I was stuck with something I needed to complete for this sprint. I had to complete some Javascript challenges called Kata's.

This challenge requires you to pass serveral "tests" and will complete once you pass all the test. I was going pretty well on the first kata but got stuck in the second.

I tried some of the solving techniques I read earlier on the sprint. It took me a while but I finally figured out why I was stuck. I kind of loose focus due to several reasons.

I was getting frustrated as I wasn't progressing as I want to be. That didn't help as my confidence was going down the longer the time I was stuck. I put pressure on myself by giving a goal to complete the challenges but with shorter timeboxes.

I ended up taking a long break (half a day) as I just realised I was actually tired from being on the screen for a long period of time. After clearing my mind and went back at it, I found that I did better and because my mind was calm and clear I managed to proceed again.

It still took me time but I manage to complete the challenge within timeframe.


Solved a problem in an elegant way.

When I returned from clearing my thoughts and having a bit of head space from the same issue above, I actually managed to get pass one test which I was stuck for more than an hour.

As soon as I started on the Kata again, I read the whole instructions again. I looked at the code given and the errors. I started to do pseudocode.

I was quite suprised how easier it was because I was well rested and more focused. I didn't have the same stress and my confidence was growing as I complete more test and eventually the whole Kata.

From this experience, I now know that I need to look after mysefl better. I just realised that I have been studying from late (10pm) at night until early morning (3 or 4am) the next day for most of the week.

This has affected my sleeping pattern which then affected my studies due to lack of sleep.






Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem solving techniques and processes:

Pseudocoding is one of the problem solving techniques we learnt from this sprint. It basically is an informal way of writing descriptions of a program or code without using the programming language.

Let's think of baking a cake, it is basically you writing your own recipe and steps for how to be bake. It doesn't need to be a full on cookbook, just the important parts. So a pseudocode way will be: gather all ingredients, prepare all utensils, make the base, make the icing, bake and put together.

Pseudocode is something I will try and practice more moving forward.

I read about the rubber ducky method. It's not something I would do out loud though. I normally talk to myself when solving problems so you can say Im also a duck.

My most preferred solving technique is probably relying on good old "google". I search for almost everything there when I get stuck. Sometimes I may not be lucky and not find anything related to what Im after.

That's the time that I will go and ask for help. I normally will try and solve problems myself and give me a timeframe. If I am still stuck then I will ask for help then, either from cohorts or my facilitator.
